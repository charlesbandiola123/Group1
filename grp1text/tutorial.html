Welcome to the Quizzler Guide!

Quizzler is your go-to platform for creating, managing, and presenting fun and educational quizzes to your audience. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to make the most of Quizzler as an educator or presenter.

Whether you're looking to engage your students in the classroom or deliver interactive knowledge checks, Quizzler has the tools for you!

Getting Started with Quizzler

First things first, let's get you set up.

1. Signing Up

If you're new to Quizzler, you'll need to create an account to start building your quizzes.

  1. Navigate to the Sign Up page. (You can also click the "Sign Up" button in the header).
  2. Fill in the required details (e.g., username, email, password).
  3. Click "Sign Up" and you're ready!

2. Logging In

If you already have an account:

  1. Go to the Log In page. (Or click the "Log In" button in the header).
  2. Enter your username and password.
  3. Click "Log In" to access your Quizzler dashboard.

Once logged in, you'll see a welcome message with your username in the header, and the "Log In" / "Sign Up" buttons will change to a "Log Out" button.

Creating Your Content

Quizzler allows you to create "Projects" (which are like sets of terms/definitions) and then generate "Quizzes" from these projects for presentation.

1. Creating a New Project (Terms & Definitions)

  1. From the main page (after logging in), click the large purple "CREATE" button. This will take you to the content creation page.
  2. Enter a Title for your project (e.g., "Chapter 1 Vocabulary", "Historical Dates").
  3. Add your terms and their corresponding definitions one by one.
    • Type a Term in the "Term" field.
    • Type its Definition in the "Definition" field.
    • Click the "Add Term" button to save it to your current project list.
  4. You'll see your added terms appear below the input fields.
  5. Once you've added all your terms, click the "Save Project" button.
Placeholder: Screenshot of `addpage.html` showing term input and project saving.

2. Generating Quizzes for Presentation

After saving a project, or by selecting a saved project, you can generate quizzes ready for display.

  1. On the content creation page (often `addpage.html` or `print.html` after loading a project), you'll typically find options to generate different quiz types.
  2. Multiple Choice Quiz:
    • Select the "Generate Multiple Choice Quiz" option (or similar).
    • Quizzler will use your terms and definitions to create multiple-choice questions formatted for presentation.
    • Save this quiz configuration with a descriptive title.
  3. Identification Quiz (Fill-in-the-blanks style):
    • Select the "Generate Identification Quiz" option (or similar).
    • This quiz type will present a definition (or term), and you can ask your audience to provide the corresponding term (or definition).
    • Save this quiz configuration.

Managing Your Projects & Quizzes

On the main page, you can view and manage your saved content, ready for presentation.

1. Viewing "My Projects"

  1. Look for a button or dropdown labeled "My Project ▼".
  2. Clicking this will display a list of all the projects (term sets) you've created and saved.
  3. Each project will be shown in a box, typically with its title.
  4. Clicking on a project box will usually load that project's terms, allowing you to review them or generate new quizzes from them.
  5. Deleting a Project: Look for a trash can icon (🗑️) or a "Delete" button next to each project. Confirm the deletion when prompted.
Placeholder: Screenshot of "My Projects" list.

2. Viewing "My Quizzes"

  1. Use the same dropdown menu (that shows "My Project ▼") and select the "My Quizzes" option. The button text might change to "My Quizzes ▼".
  2. This will display a list of all the quizzes you've generated and saved.
  3. Each quiz will be shown in a box, typically with its title.
  4. Clicking on a quiz box will open that quiz in a presentation mode (e.g., redirecting you to `multiplechoice.html` or `identification.html`), ready for you to display to your audience.
  5. Deleting a Quiz: Similar to projects, look for a trash can icon (🗑️) or a "Delete" button next to each quiz. Confirm deletion.

Presenting Your Quiz

Once you've selected a quiz from "My Quizzes," Quizzler will prepare it for presentation to your students or audience.

  1. The quiz will load (e.g., on `multiplechoice.html` or `identification.html`), displaying one question at a time.
  2. Displaying Questions:
    • Project this screen for your audience to see.
    • Read the question and options (for multiple choice) aloud.
    • For identification questions, present the definition/term and ask for the corresponding answer.
  3. Audience Participation: Students can answer verbally, by raising hands, writing on paper, or using any classroom response system you prefer. Quizzler itself does not collect answers.
  4. Navigating Through Questions:
    • Use "Next Question," "Previous Question," or similar controls provided on the page to move through the quiz.
    • Some quizzes might have an option to reveal the correct answer after discussing the question.
  5. Ending the Presentation: Once you've gone through all questions, you can simply close the quiz or navigate back to your dashboard.

The goal is to facilitate an interactive session where you control the pace and discussion around each question.

Tips for Using Quizzler Effectively

Be Specific with Titles: When creating projects and quizzes, use clear and descriptive titles. This makes them easier to find for your presentation sessions.

Prepare for Discussion: Think about potential discussion points or clarifications for each question as you'll be guiding the session.

Test Your Setup: Before presenting to an audience, do a quick run-through to ensure your display (projector, shared screen) works correctly and you're familiar with the navigation.

Vary Quiz Types: Use both Multiple Choice and Identification formats to keep your presentations engaging and test different types of knowledge.

Pace Yourself: Adjust the pace of presenting questions based on your audience's engagement and the complexity of the material.

Adjust Browser Zoom for Presentation: When presenting your quiz, use your browser's zoom feature (usually Ctrl/Cmd and `+` or `-`) to adjust the size of the content on screen. This helps ensure the quiz questions and options are clearly visible and fit well on your specific display or projector setup.
